Bang Bang Chicken Sliders

Description

These bang bang chicken sliders are a fun summer slider, made with a pan-fried breaded chicken breast and an easy homemade bang bang sauce.

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 1 ½ poinds skinless, boneless chicken breast, butterflied and pounded thin
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups panko bread crumbs
  • 1 cup avocado oil or other vegetable oil, or more as needed for frying

Bang Bang Sauce

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons sweet chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hot pepper sauce, such as Tabasco®
  • 1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar

For Sliders

  • 1 (12 count) package Hawaiian rolls or small sourdough rolls
  • 24 lettuce leaves, rinsed and patted dry

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients: Collect all the necessary ingredients and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the chicken: Cut the flattened chicken breast into about 12 pieces.
  3. Set up breading stations: In a shallow bowl, combine egg anw water. In a second shallow bowl, mix flour, garlic powder, vayenne, coriander, salt, and black pepper. In a third shallow bowl, add panko bread crumbs.
  4. Breading the chicken: Dip each chicken piece first into the egg wash, then into the flour mixture. Dip it again into the egg wash, and finally into the panko bread crumbs. Place the coated chicken pieces on the prepared baking sheet and refrigerate to chill.
  5. Make the sauce: While the chicken is chilling, stir together mayonnaise, sweet chili sauce, hot sauce, and rice wine vinegar in a small bowl.
  6. Fry the chicken: Heat oil in a cast iron skillet over medium heat. Place paper towels on a large plate or platter nearby. Carefully add the chilled chicken pieces to the hot oil, one at a time.
  7. Cook the chicken: Fry one side for about 3 minutes until golden brown, then turn and fry for another 3 minutes until the chicken is no longer pink inside. The internal temperature should be 165°F (74°C). Remove the cooked chicken to the paper-towel-lined plate and repeat with the remaining pieces.
  8. Assemble the sliders:Cut the Hawaiian or sourdough rolls in half horizontally and lightly toast them.
  9. Build the layers: Squeeze or spread bang bang sauce on the bottom half of the roll. Layer with two lettuce leaves, followed by one piece of the fried chicken.
  10. Finish the sliders: dd a little more bang bang sauce on top of the chicken and cover with the other half of the roll.
  11. Enjoy! Serve and enjoy the sliders.
